THE CITY

Barcelona, the cosmopolitan capital of Spain’s Catalonia region, is known for its art and architecture. The city of Barcelona is located at a strategic geographical point, on the Mediterranean coast of the Iberian Peninsula, protected by the Collserola mountain range, bounded by two river deltas and surrounded by a privileged nature. This has led to its sustainable growth: the city has become a large European metropolis integrated into its surroundings.

CULTURE EXPERIENCES

SAGRADA FAMILIA

The Basílica de la Sagrada Família, also known as the Sagrada Família, is a large unfinished Roman Catholic minor basilica in Barcelona, Catalonia, Spain. Designed by Catalan architect Antoni Gaudí, his work on the building is part of a UNESCO World Heritage Site.


LAS RAMBLAS

It is the most famous street and one of the most essential places to visit in Barcelona. This walk full of kiosks, flower stands, terraces of bars and restaurants, artists and street painters, is always full of people and activity, so any time of the day is ideal to enjoy it.



BARRIO GÓTICO

It is the oldest neighborhood in Barcelona, with an origin that is situated in the Roman era from which you can still see some remains of the ancient city of Barcino. During the medieval period, several Gothic-style buildings and palaces were built, which together with its narrow streets, local shops and tapas bars, make it a perfect neighborhood for walking.


EL PASSEIG DE GRACIA

This street connects the popular Plaza de Catalunya with the Gracia neighborhood and stands out for its modernist buildings, restaurants and its stores of the most famous and luxurious brands. Among the jewels of Catalan modernism of Paseo de Gracia, Casa Batlló or La Pedrera stands out.



MONTJUIC

The mountain of Montjuic, located in front of the sea and with a height of 173 meters, has become one of the best viewpoints of Barcelona and an ideal place for leisure, culture and sports activities. From the Plaza España, crossing the Venetian towers, you can access the Magic Fountain of Montjuic, where a light and water show takes place at night.


LA BARCELONETA

It is one of the mythical neighborhoods of Barcelona, located next to the sea, which still retains the marine charm in its narrow streets and its restaurants of fresh fish, seafood and paella.
